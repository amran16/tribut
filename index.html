Dr. Hawa Abdi

An activist and a physician

Here is the background of Dr. Hawa Abdi's life:

  • 1947 - Hawa was born in Mogadishu, Somalia. Here mother died when she was only 12 and took on the family chores as the eldest child in the family.
  • 1964 - She received a sholarship from Soviet Union. Hawa studied medicine.
  • 1971 - Hawa graduated and began law studies at Mogadishu in the follwoing year.
  • 1973 - Hawa got married and gave birth to her first child two years later.
  • 1983 - Hawa opened the Rural Health Development Organisation (RHDO). It began as a one-room clinic offering free medical services in the rural area.
  • 1991 - civil war broke out and Hawa stayed behind. She was advised to use her qualification to assist the volnerable.
  • 2007 - RHDO was rename to Dr. Hawa Abdi Foundation.
  • 2012 - Hawa temporarily suspended services when the Somali militants gained power. In this year Hawa was nominated for the Nobel Peace Prize.
  • 2014 - Abdi received the Roosevelt Four Freedoms Award in Netherlands
  • Dr. Hawa Abdi Foundtion (DHAF) is run by Hawa and her two physician daughters, Deeqa and Amina, who followed in their mother's footsteps as bo/gyn specialist.
